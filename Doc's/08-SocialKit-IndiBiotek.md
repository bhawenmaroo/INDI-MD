## 1. Platform Recommendations

Based on IndiBiotek's multi-segment audience — farmers, doctors, lab purchase officers, VCs, and grant bodies — three platforms are recommended. Quality over quantity: three well-managed accounts outperform six neglected ones.

| Platform | Why IndiBiotek Needs It | Primary Audience Reached |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | VCs, grant bodies, institutional buyers, and purchase officers spend significant time here. Best platform for B2B credibility, patent announcements, R&D updates, and partnership news. | VCs, grant bodies, corporate purchase officers, researchers |
| **Instagram** | Visual storytelling for the brand — lab shots, farm field visuals, product features. Growing biotech and agri-science community in India. | Younger purchase officers, agri-enthusiasts, science community |
| **Facebook** | Farmer communities in India are active on Facebook. Agri division content — biofertilizer tips, crop protection guides — performs well here. | Farmers, rural agri buyers, local distributors |

**Google Business Profile** (recommended): Set up a free Google Business listing for IndiBiotek — Kolkata headquarters. This helps the company appear in local searches and adds a star-rating trust signal for any visitor who Googles the company name.

## 2. Brand Voice on Social

IndiBiotek's website tone is balanced — authoritative on science, warm on mission, bold on vision. On social, the tone shifts slightly:

- **LinkedIn:** Professional, confident, thought-leadership. Write like a founder who knows their field deeply but explains it accessibly. No jargon without explanation.
- **Instagram:** Visual-first, energetic, proud of the mission. Captions are shorter, more punchy. Emojis used sparingly but effectively.
- **Facebook:** Approachable and direct. Farmer-facing content uses simple, benefit-first language. Urban content is more brand-focused.

**Never:** corporate-speak, generic biotech clichés, or copy that could belong to any company. Every post should feel unmistakably IndiBiotek.

## 3. Content Pillars

IndiBiotek posts across four content pillars. Each pillar serves a different audience and purpose.

### Pillar 1 — Behind the Scenes (30% of posts)
*What's happening at IndiBiotek — real, human, transparent*

Content ideas:
- Lab work at SRM University C4D or Adamas E-YUVA Centre
- Team introductions and founder story
- Day-in-the-life at the R&D facility
- Milestone celebrations — patent filing, BIRAC acceptance, new catalogue launch
- Behind-the-scenes of product development

Why this pillar: Investors and grant bodies want to see that the company is real, active, and moving. Buyers trust companies they can see working.

---

### Pillar 2 — Education & Science (30% of posts)
*Teaching the audience something valuable about biotech, agri-science, or diagnostics*

Content ideas:
- "What is CRISPR and why does it matter for diagnostics in India?"
- "Why biofertilizers outperform chemical fertilizers for Indian soil"
- "The difference between ELISA, CLIA, and Rapid Test kits — explained simply"
- "How LAMP technology works — and why it's faster than PCR"
- "What Make in India means for Indian healthcare"

Why this pillar: Positions IndiBiotek as a thought leader. Educating buyers builds trust before they ever make a purchase.

---

### Pillar 3 — Product & Division Spotlights (25% of posts)
*Showcasing IndiBiotek's products and catalogues*

Content ideas:
- Product category features — one product type per post
- Catalogue launch announcements ("Our Agri Catalogue is now live — 11 products, one link")
- Comparison posts — "IndiBiotek vs. Imported: why domestic matters"
- R&D milestone posts — "Patent filed. Here's what it means."
- Division-specific posts tagged to the right audience (#lifesciences #agri #scientific)

Why this pillar: Direct pipeline for purchase officer inquiries and catalogue downloads.

---

### Pillar 4 — Mission & Impact (15% of posts)
*The bigger story — why IndiBiotek exists*

Content ideas:
- India's import dependency in diagnostics — the problem we're solving
- Celebrating government certifications — Startup India, MSME, Make in India
- Partnership announcements — SRM University, Adamas University
- Impact statements — "Indian labs shouldn't pay import prices for Indian science"
- Responses to national events — National Science Day, World Environment Day

Why this pillar: The mission content is what VCs and grant bodies share. It also differentiates IndiBiotek from catalogue-only competitors.

## 5. Visual Layout Guide

### LinkedIn Post Format
- **Dimensions:** 1200 × 627px (landscape) for link posts; 1080 × 1080px for standalone image posts
- **Layout zones:** Top 40% — visual/graphic or photo. Bottom 60% — headline text overlay or left to caption.
- **Colours:** Deep Navy `#0A1628` background for branded graphics. BioGreen `#00C896` as accent on text or borders. White Poppins text.
- **Caption structure:** Hook line (1 sentence) → 3–4 lines of body → 1 CTA line → 3–5 hashtags

Example caption structure:
```
India imports 80% of its diagnostic kits. We're changing that.

IndiBiotek's Lifesciences division is developing point-of-care
diagnostic kits entirely in India — backed by BIRAC and filed
under Patent No. 202511077832.

Explore our Lifesciences catalogue → [link in bio]

#MakeInIndia #Biotechnology #Diagnostics #StartupIndia #IndiBiotek
```

---

### Instagram Post Format
- **Dimensions:** 1080 × 1350px (portrait, 4:5 ratio) — performs best in feed
- **Layout zones:** Full bleed visual. BioGreen accent strip at bottom (80px) with IndiBiotek wordmark.
- **Caption structure:** Hook emoji + 1 punchy line → 2–3 lines of body → CTA → line break → hashtag block (10–15 tags)

Example caption structure:
```
🧬 Science made in India.

Our ASSURED device combines LAMP, microfluidics, and CRISPR
technology — all in one portable diagnostic platform.

Link in bio to browse our Lifesciences catalogue.

#IndiBiotek #Biotech #MadeInIndia #Diagnostics #LifeSciences
#StartupIndia #BIRAC #IndianScience #BiotechIndia #PointOfCare
```

---

### Facebook Post Format
- **Dimensions:** 1200 × 630px (landscape) for shared image posts
- **Caption structure:** 2–3 short paragraphs. No jargon for farmer-facing content. CTA with website link.

Example farmer-facing caption structure:
```
Are your crops losing yield despite heavy fertilizer use?

IndiBiotek's biofertilizers work with your soil — not against it.
Natural, science-backed, and safe for long-term use.

Browse our Agri catalogue: www.indibiotek.com/agri
Contact us: +91 89020 52927
```

## 10. Competitor Social Snapshot

Based on research across the Indian biotech sector:

- Most Indian biotech companies have **weak or inactive social media** — irregular posting, no visual identity consistency, heavy on press release reposts.
- No Indian biotech startup in this segment owns the **education + mission** combination on social.
- International players (Merck, ThermoFisher) dominate LinkedIn with high-production content — but they have no Indian voice or Make in India angle.

**IndiBiotek's opportunity:** Be the most consistent, visually premium, and mission-driven Indian biotech voice on LinkedIn and Instagram. The bar is low. The opportunity is significant.
